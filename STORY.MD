# The Infinite Descent

## High Concept

In the near future, humanity receives a message from an ancient interdimensional civilization known as **The Curators**.

They claim that reality itself is dying.

Every intelligent species eventually reaches a point where it becomes stagnant, predictable, and incapable of adapting to the challenges ahead. To identify which civilizations are capable of surviving the coming collapse, the Curators administer a test:

# The Infinite Descent

A single dungeon appears beneath the Earth.

Every human is eligible to enter.

Anyone who steps through one of the dungeon's gateways becomes part of the Run.

The rules are simple:

- One life.
- No respawns.
- Reach the exit before the floor timer expires.
- The next floor does not begin until the current floor closes.
- Every participant worldwide shares the same dungeon progression.
- Death is permanent.

The prize?

Nobody knows.

The Curators refuse to explain what awaits on Floor 100.

# Themes

## Adaptation

The dungeon rewards change, creativity, and growth.

## Identity

Abilities are not chosen. They emerge from behavior.

## Trust

Friendly fire is enabled. Every alliance carries risk.

## Sacrifice

Permanent death gives weight to every decision.

## Evolution

The dungeon is constantly observing, learning, and reshaping players.

---

# Act I: The Invitation

## The Arrival

Without warning, strange black doors begin appearing across the world.

When touched, each door delivers the same message directly into the person's mind:

> Humanity has been selected.

Most people assume it is a hoax.

Then people begin entering.

Some never return.

Others emerge with impossible powers.

Within weeks:

- Governments attempt to regulate access.
- Religious groups debate the meaning of the dungeon.
- News networks cover every floor completion.
- Millions become obsessed with watching dungeon runs.

The first floor becomes the most viewed event in human history.

---

## Discovery of the Rules

Early participants learn:

- Death is permanent.
- The dungeon timer is real.
- Loot is real.
- Abilities are real.
- No predefined classes exist.

The world realizes this is not a game.

It is reality.

---

# Act II: The Dungeon Learns

## Emergent Classes

Players initially search for traditional RPG classes.

There are none.

Instead, the dungeon observes behavior.

Examples:

### The Protector

A player who repeatedly shields allies begins developing defensive abilities.

### The Hunter

A player who specializes in tracking enemies gains perception and pursuit skills.

### The Shadow

A player who prefers stealth develops concealment powers.

### The Negotiator

A player who solves problems through diplomacy gains influence-based abilities.

### The Berserker

A player who embraces violence develops increasingly destructive powers.

No two players become exactly alike.

The dungeon is not assigning classes.

The dungeon is profiling personalities.

---

## Global Obsession

As floors advance:

- Professional teams form.
- Streamers become celebrities.
- Sponsorships emerge.
- Entire economies develop around dungeon information.

Every floor completion becomes a global event.

---

# Act III: Humanity Divides

As progression continues, philosophical factions emerge.

## The Shepherds

Mission:

Protect as many people as possible.

Beliefs:

- Every life matters.
- Strength exists to protect others.
- Humanity wins together.

---

## The Ascendants

Mission:

Advance only the strongest.

Beliefs:

- Weak players consume resources.
- Survival proves worth.
- Evolution requires hardship.

---

## The Gamblers

Mission:

Pursue rare rewards regardless of risk.

Beliefs:

- Great rewards require great risks.
- Safety is stagnation.
- Every floor should be exploited for maximum gain.

---

## The Collectors

Mission:

Understand the dungeon.

Beliefs:

- Knowledge is the most valuable resource.
- Hidden systems matter more than power.
- The dungeon has secrets deeper than loot.

---

## The Cult of Floor 100

Mission:

Reach the final floor.

Beliefs:

- The dungeon is divine.
- Floor 100 contains ultimate truth.
- Ascension awaits those who survive.

---

# Act IV: The Hidden Truth

## Ancient Records

Around Floors 30-40, players uncover forgotten archives.

The discovery changes everything.

The Curators did not create the dungeon.

They inherited it.

---

## The Revelation

Thousands of civilizations have entered before humanity.

None reached the end.

Entire species disappeared attempting the Descent.

The Curators are no longer judges.

They are observers.

Nobody truly understands the dungeon anymore.

---

# Act V: Transformation

## The Nature of Power

Players begin intentionally shaping themselves.

A profound realization spreads:

> Every choice changes who you become.

Examples:

- A healer who embraces violence gradually loses restorative abilities.
- A coward who repeatedly sacrifices for others becomes courageous.
- A selfish survivor who learns teamwork gains leadership powers.

Abilities reflect identity.

The dungeon rewards authenticity.

---

## Humanity Evolves

As floors progress:

- Cultures change.
- Governments lose influence.
- Dungeon veterans become legends.
- New social classes emerge based on dungeon accomplishments.

Humanity is no longer the same species that entered Floor 1.

---

# Endgame

## Floor 100

The surviving players finally uncover the truth.

The dungeon is not a test.

It is a machine.

A machine that sustains reality itself.

Each civilization that entered contributed its experiences, knowledge, and identity to the system.

The machine has reached its limits.

Reality is beginning to collapse.

---

## The Final Choice

The survivors learn:

One civilization must become the next caretaker.

The winners do not receive wealth.

They do not receive immortality.

They do not receive paradise.

Instead, they inherit responsibility.

To save reality, someone must become the next Curators.

Doing so means giving up their humanity forever.

---

- Cyberpunk metropolis
